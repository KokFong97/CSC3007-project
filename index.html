Visualization 1

Gun deaths in Florida

Data

  • Interval: Time series from 1990 and beyond

  • Ratio: Number of murders committed using firearms

  • Ratio: Highlighted area represent level of deaths
Idiom

  • Line graph representing a trend

  • The graph is also an area chart

  • X and Y-Axis for number of murder as years goes on

  • Graph is inverted with the y-axis starting from the top
Criticisms

  • Graph is misleading due to graph being inverted. User wouldn't know its inverted and there is red/white misconception.

  • X-axis at the bottom doesnt help at all in an inverted chart. It will only mislead the users more.

  • X-axis shouldn't be a range

  • Annotation will lead the users to think the red is the background.

  • The idiom used is confusing. What does the red area encodes?
Criticisms

  • Intended story telling is not conveyed to the users. Due to how the "red" dont look like blood

  • It’s a very unique graph that allows viewers to have many ways to look at it, to the point it sparked political arguments on the web. It was widely derided as bad, misleading, and politically motivated.

Visualization 1 Improvements

Improvements

A better way to do it

  • Have a more representative title

  • X-axis at the top to orientate the chart clearly

  • Having a barchart would be a better representation of blood dripping than line chart

Visualization 2

US and China in Philippine Foreign Trade

Data

  • Nominal: US & China

  • Interval: Time series from 2000 to 2012

  • Ratio: Share in Philippine Imports

  • Ratio: Share in Philippine Exports

Idiom

  • A connected scatterplot

  • X and Y-Axis for Imports and Exports

  • Norminal color scale for US and China

  • Labelled zones (More Imports/Exports)
Criticisms

  • A connected scatterplot is uncommon and can be unintuitive.

  • We are wired to read time from left to right. Time goes in all directions here.

  • Connected scatterplot can be very messy, and trends can be hard to see.

  • A connected scatterplot is an alternative for dual-axis charts with different scales.

  • However, the axes here are using the same scale.
Criticisms

  • The dotted line can be mistaken as a trend line.

  • The Axes are labelled % twice.

  • The labelled zones are redundant.

Visualization 2 Improvements

Improvements

  • Can be represented better with a line chart
  • Either use units in the axis or each label in axis
  • There should be a legend for the colours to denote the countries instead of writing it in the chart
  • The word “more exports” and “more imports” at the top left and bottom right of the chart serves no purpose and can be removed
